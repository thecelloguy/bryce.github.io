Bryce L Lunt

Doctoral Candidate, Graham F. Hatfull Lab, Department of Biological Sciences, University of Pittsburgh

Expertise: Microbiology and Molecular Biology

Dissertation Research:

Many mycobacterium phages can stably integrate their DNA into the host chromosome. A protein called integrase catalyzes a site-specific-recombination reaction, recombining the bacterial and phage DNA. I am exploring a new integration system from the mycobacterium phage Brujita, which differs greatly from other known integrase systems. Specifically, Brujita Integrase lacks the major components of traditional tyrosine integrase systems (such as arm-type protein-DNA interactions), yet retains full functionality and directionality control. These unique features give Brujita Integrase the potential to be a powerful biotechnological tool and also offer important insights into the evolution of more complex integration systems.
